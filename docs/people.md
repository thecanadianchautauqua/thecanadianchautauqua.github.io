# People of Niagara-on-the-Lake & Chautauqua

Biographical sketches of figures who shaped the town and the Chautauqua neighbourhood. Source keys match the [reference list](../references/references.md).

---

## Founders, governors & soldiers

### Col. John Butler (1728–1796)
Loyalist officer and commander of **Butler's Rangers**, the provincial corps whose disbanded soldiers and refugee families founded the settlement in **1781**. The town's first name, **Butlersburg**, honoured him. **[W][Br]**

### John Graves Simcoe (1752–1806)
First **Lieutenant-Governor of Upper Canada**. He renamed the town **Newark** and made it the province's **first capital** in **1792**, convening the first Parliament at **Navy Hall**. At Newark in **1793** he championed the **Act Against Slavery**, an early step toward abolition in the British Empire. He moved the capital to York in 1797. **[W][CE]**

### John McFarland (c. 1752–1815)
Scottish-born "King's boat builder," granted land by **George III**, who built **McFarland House** (c. 1800), one of the few structures to survive the War of 1812 (it served as a hospital for both armies). **[Niagara Falls Tourism]**

## War of 1812 heroine

### Laura Secord (1775–1868)
Resident of the Niagara frontier who, during the American occupation of 1813, **walked ~32 km** through enemy-held country to warn British forces of a planned attack, contributing to the British/Indigenous victory at the **Battle of Beaver Dams.** Her one-storey house stood nearby (her Queenston home, 1803–1835, is a museum); she is the namesake of the Canadian chocolate brand. **[W]** Her record is held at the [NOTL Museum](https://niagarahistorical.pastperfectonline.com/byperson?keyword=Secord,+Laura).

## Chroniclers & culture

### William Kirby (1817–1906)
England-born writer who settled in Niagara, edited the **Niagara Mail (1850–1871)**, and served as **Collector of Customs (1871–1895)**. He wrote the classic Canadian historical romance ***The Golden Dog* (Le Chien d'Or, 1877)** — fifteen years in the writing — and the local history ***Annals of Niagara* (1896)**. A **National Historic Person**; his Niagara home, *The Lawn*, survives. **[Br: Kirby][DCB][PC]**

### Janet Carnochan (1839–1926)
Niagara schoolteacher, historian, and driving force of local heritage preservation. She **founded the Niagara Historical Society in 1895** and was its **first president, 1895–1925**, raising some **$5,000** for a museum. She led the building of **Memorial Hall** (begun 1906, **opened 1907**) — **the first building in Ontario designed solely as a historical museum** — the core of today's **Niagara-on-the-Lake Museum.** She wrote and edited numerous works, including a *History of Niagara*; a provincial plaque at Memorial Hall honours her. **[Janet Carnochan — Wikipedia][NOTL Museum]**

## Chautauqua resort figures

### James Crooks (1778–1860) — *"the Honourable James Crooks"*
Born in **Kilmarnock, Ayrshire, Scotland** (15 April 1778), son of a shoemaker; emigrated **1791** to join his half-brother **Francis**, who had founded a mercantile house at **Fort Niagara (NY)** around 1788. With his brother **William**, James ran the prominent Niagara firm **W. & J. Crooks** — military provisioning, grain and flour shipping, brewing, distilling, and potash — and built the schooner *Lord Nelson* at Niagara in 1811. His Mississauga Point estate, **Crookston**, occupied the land that became the Chautauqua grounds; his house was bombarded at the opening of the **Battle of Fort George (27 May 1813)**. He never rebuilt, resettling on 400 acres in **West Flamborough** (bought from Elizabeth Russell), where he created **Crooks' Hollow** and built the **first paper mill in Upper Canada (1826)**, dying there in 1860. Member of the Legislative Council; his heirs sold the Niagara land in 1886. **[DCB: Crooks][James Crooks — Wikipedia][Flamborough Archives]**

> **"James vs William" resolved:** the land is tied to the firm **W. & J. Crooks** (the brothers William and James, plus half-brother Francis). The secondary "William Crooks, 1808 land grant" claim reflects the firm/family, but the estate-holder who relocated to Crooks' Hollow is **James Crooks**. The trail runs **Scotland → Fort Niagara (NY) → Niagara → Hamilton** — three of the project's spokes in one family. See [external-archives-source-map.md](external-archives-source-map.md).

### Robert M. Warren
**Postmaster of Niagara** who, with Toronto associates, conceived and led the **Niagara Assembly**, founding the Chautauqua summer resort on the former Crooks property in **1886**. His portrait is held by the NOTL Museum. **[NHR][EN][GAC]**

### John Readhead
Chautauqua resident who operated the resort's **Boat and Bath Houses** on the beach. **[NHR]**

### The Niagara Assembly board
Beyond Robert Warren, the museum's plan record (986.088) names the Assembly's founders and directors — a roster of Methodist clergy, educators, and Toronto businessmen typical of the Chautauqua movement: **Charles D. Warren**; **Rev. W. H. Withrow**; **John N. Lake**; **Edward Gurney**; **James L. Hughes**; **William Houston**; **Rev. John McEwen**; **J. J. MacLaren**; and **Lewis C. Peake**. The 1894 survey adds **James George Currie** and **William Bell**. **[NOTL Museum 986.088, 2000.027]** Several were nationally prominent — they are documented far from NOTL (see [external-mentions.md](external-mentions.md)):

- **William Henry Withrow (1839–1908)** — Toronto-born Methodist minister, **editor of the *Canadian Methodist Magazine* (1874–1906)** and a prolific author; the movement's literary-religious establishment in person. **[DCB: Withrow]**
- **James Laughlin Hughes (1846–1935)** — Toronto's **chief public-school inspector (1874–1913)** and a champion of the **kindergarten** — a neat echo of the grounds' **Froebel Avenue**. **[Canadian Encyclopedia; DCB]**
- **Edward Gurney** — of the **E. & C. Gurney** stove foundry (Hamilton → Toronto). The firm's founder Edward Gurney died in 1884, so the Assembly's member is likely his **son and successor, Edward Gurney Jr.** **[DCB: Gurney]** *(generation to verify)*
- **John James MacLaren** — Toronto barrister, later **Justice of the Ontario Court of Appeal**, a Methodist layman and temperance leader; his papers form a fonds at **Victoria University Archives (U of T)**. **[Victoria University Archives F2102]**
- **John Neilson Lake (1834–1925)** — Methodist preacher and temperance activist who, as commissioner of the **Temperance Colonization Society**, **chose the site of and founded the city of Saskatoon** in 1882 — a remarkable reach from the Niagara grounds to the Saskatchewan prairie. **[John Neilson Lake — Wikipedia]**
- **William Houston** — Toronto journalist and educationist, **Librarian of the Ontario Legislative Assembly**, editor of *Documents Illustrative of the Canadian Constitution* (1891). **[William Houston — Wikipedia; DCB]**

### Brian Doherty (1906–1974) — founder of the Shaw Festival
NOTL lawyer and theatre visionary who **founded the Shaw Festival in 1962**, mounting two George Bernard Shaw plays over eight summer weekends with director **Maynard Burgess** and ten unpaid actors. His and others' recollections are preserved in the NOTL Heritage Portal's **Shaw Festival Oral History** series (alongside co-founder **Calvin Rand**, longtime artistic director **Christopher Newton**, actor **Paxton Whitehead**, and more). **[NOTL Heritage Portal]**

### George Bernard Shaw (1856–1950) — *namesake*
The Irish playwright is not a NOTL figure, but the **Shaw Festival** (from 1962) that bears his name has shaped the modern town and the Chautauqua neighbourhood (nicknamed **"SHAWtauqua"** for the festival staff who live there). **[W][NHR]**
