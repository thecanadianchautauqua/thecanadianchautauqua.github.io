# Events — Battles, Fires, Fairs & Festivals

Notable dated events in the life of Niagara-on-the-Lake and Chautauqua. Source keys match the [reference list](../references/references.md). See also the [master timeline](timeline.md).

## War & frontier conflict

### First Parliament of Upper Canada (17 September 1792)
The province's first legislature convened at **Navy Hall**, beginning Newark's five-session run as the capital and producing landmark laws including the **1793 Act Against Slavery.** **[W][PC]**

### Battle of Fort George (27 May 1813)
After a two-day bombardment from Fort Niagara and the U.S. fleet, American forces captured **Fort George** and the town, beginning a roughly **seven-month occupation.** The opening bombardment wrecked **James Crooks' estate at Crookston**, the future Chautauqua grounds. **[W][NHR][GAC]**

### Laura Secord's walk (June 1813)
During the occupation, **Laura Secord** walked ~32 km to warn the British of a planned American attack, aiding the victory at **Beaver Dams.** **[W]**

### Burning of Niagara (10 December 1813)
Retreating American troops **burned the town to the ground**, turning residents out into a snowstorm — one of the war's most notorious acts on the Niagara frontier. **[W]**

### Capture of Fort Niagara (19 December 1813)
British forces crossed the river and **stormed Fort Niagara** in retaliation, holding it to the war's end. **[W]**

## The Chautauqua resort era

### Chautauqua seasons & excursions (1887–1894)
The **Niagara Assembly's** summer assembly opened — sources give an **11-day inaugural season in summer 1887** and/or the **1888 season** coinciding with the Hotel Chautauqua's opening; the **1890 season opened 14 June and ran 12 days.** Programmes were **inter-denominational and educational** (missionary addresses, CLSC study, music). Daily steamers and Michigan Central trains brought visitors mid-June to mid-September, and the **New York Central ran advertised excursion trains "To the Canadian Chautauqua" from Buffalo** (July 1890), while **church Sunday-school groups** (e.g. Buffalo's Dearborn Street Baptist) made it an **annual picnic destination** (1891–92). **[GAC][EN][NHR][NYS Historic Newspapers; see [external-mentions.md](external-mentions.md)]**

### Bankruptcy of the Niagara Assembly (1894)
Weak lot sales, too few accommodations, and financial troubles forced the Assembly into **bankruptcy**; the **Niagara Syndicate** took over and renamed the hotel the **Strathcona.** **[NHR][NN2]**

### Y.M.C.A. international student conference (June 1908)
The Hotel Strathcona hosted a notable international student gathering in its final years. **[GAC]**

### Strathcona Hotel fire (27 August 1909)
The hotel **burned down** — reportedly from an exploding lamp in the servants' basement; guests escaped by jumping from balconies. The owners did **not rebuild**, ending the resort era. **[NN2][NHR]** The fire was newsworthy **across New York State**: the *Northern Tribune* (Gouverneur) reported "Strathcona Hotel Burned Down," and the Richfield Springs papers carried it too. **[NYS Historic Newspapers]**

### Mississauga Land Beach subdivision (1922)
The **Mississauga Land Beach Company** subdivided and sold the remaining resort lots, turning Chautauqua into the **residential neighbourhood** it is today. **[GAC]**

## Modern town

### 8th World Scout Jamboree (1955)
NOTL hosted **11,000+ Scouts from 71 countries**, one of the largest gatherings in the town's history. **[W]**

### Founding of the Shaw Festival (1962)
NOTL lawyer **Brian Doherty**, director **Maynard Burgess**, and ten unpaid actors staged **two George Bernard Shaw plays over eight summer weekends** at the **Court House Theatre** — the birth of the **Shaw Festival**, now a defining annual season (April–October) and a major economic and cultural force. **[NOTL Heritage Portal][W]** *(1962 first season; formal incorporation later in the 1960s–70s — verify)*

### Heritage designations (1986 / 1996 / 2003)
Old Town named an Ontario **Heritage Conservation District (1986)**, **"Prettiest Town in Canada" (1996)**, and a **National Historic Site of Canada (2003).** **[W][PC]**

### Recurring events today
- **Shaw Festival** season (spring–fall)
- **Doors Open Niagara-on-the-Lake** — heritage buildings opened to the public. **[Niagara Now]**
- **Niagara Jazz Festival**, wine-country and culinary events across the season.
