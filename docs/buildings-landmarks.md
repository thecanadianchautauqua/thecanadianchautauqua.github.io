# Buildings & Landmarks of Niagara-on-the-Lake

Forts, churches, public buildings, houses, and the Chautauqua resort structures. Dates are construction dates unless noted. Source keys match the [reference list](../references/references.md).

---

## Military & government

### Navy Hall (1765 / rebuilt)
Originally a **Royal Navy establishment built 1765** on the riverbank. It housed the **first Parliament of Upper Canada** from **17 September 1792** through June 1796 while Newark was the capital. Damaged in the War of 1812; the surviving stone building is a National Historic Site. **[Navy Hall — Wikipedia][PC]**

### Fort George (1796–1799)
British fort built south of the town after Fort Niagara was ceded to the U.S. Captured by the Americans at the **Battle of Fort George (May 1813)**, retaken later that year. Reconstructed in the 1930s and now a **Parks Canada National Historic Site.** **[W][PC: Fort George]**

### Fort Mississauga (1813–1816)
Built at **Mississauga Point** — beside the future Chautauqua grounds — from brick salvaged from the burned town, on the site of the former lighthouse. Its star-shaped earthworks and central brick tower survive. **[W]**

### Niagara District Court House (1847)
Imposing Queen Street public building, built **1847** out of range of Fort Niagara's guns; later a Parks Canada headquarters and a **Shaw Festival** theatre. **[W]**

## Churches

### St. Mark's Church (parish 1791; church 1809; rebuilt 1822–1828)
Anglican parish founded **1791**; its **sandstone church completed 1809** is among the oldest in Ontario. Used as a **barracks and military hospital** and burned by retreating Americans in **December 1813**; **rebuilt 1822–1826 and consecrated 1828.** Its churchyard holds Loyalist-era graves. **[St. Mark's — Wikipedia][EN]**

## Houses

### McFarland House (c. 1800)
Georgian brick house built **c. 1800** by **John McFarland** and sons on a George III land grant; one of the few pre-1812 buildings in town, used as a **hospital by both armies** during the war. Now a heritage museum in a riverside park. **[Niagara Falls Tourism]**

### McDougal-Harrison House (c. 1820) & others
Among the cluster of early-19th-century Georgian and Regency homes rebuilt after 1813 that give Queen and King Streets their character. **[W]**

### Laura Secord's house
The heroine's modest one-storey home (her Queenston residence **1803–1835** is preserved as a museum). **[W]**

## Maritime & civic

### Mississauga Point Lighthouse (1804)
The **first lighthouse on the Great Lakes**, built **1804** at Mississauga Point; demolished c. 1814 so **Fort Mississauga** could be built on the spot. **[W]**

### Niagara Public School (1859)
The town's **first public school**, built **1859.** **[W]**

### Niagara-on-the-Lake Museum / Memorial Hall (1907)
**Memorial Hall (1907)** was Ontario's **first purpose-built museum**, founded by the **Niagara Historical Society** (Janet Carnochan). Today's **NOTL Museum** is the principal repository of the town's and Chautauqua's photographs and archives. **[NOTL Museum]** *(date to verify)*

## Chautauqua resort structures (1886–1909)

### Hotel Chautauqua → Hotel Strathcona (1888–1909)
A **three-storey resort hotel** opened **1888** with electric light, telegraph, telephone, baths, a barbershop, and a grand dining room. Renamed the **Hotel Strathcona** under the Niagara Syndicate; **destroyed by fire on 27 August 1909** and never rebuilt. **[GAC][NN2]**

### The Amphitheatre (c. 1886–1888)
A **~4,000-seat** open timber amphitheatre at the centre of the wheel, inside today's **Circle Street**, used for lectures, concerts, and divine services. A **verified interior photograph survives** — exposed roof trusses over tiered wooden benches, a decorated stage with a piano, Union Jack, and "CLSC" banner, captioned *"AMPHITHEATRE (SEATING CAPACITY 4,000)"* — but **no exterior photograph is known**; the building's fan-shaped footprint appears at the hub of the grounds map. **[EN][NN2][GAC; [photos/FINDINGS-amphitheatre.md](../photos/FINDINGS-amphitheatre.md)]**

### Lakeview Boarding House / Lakeside House
The resort's second, smaller hotel. **[NHR][GAC]**

### Wharf, rail spur & generating plant
A **steamship wharf** (for the *Cibola* and *Chicora*), a **Michigan Central Railway** spur, and an **electric generating plant** served the grounds. **[NHR][GAC]**

### Surviving cottages
Original Chautauqua cottages remain in use; locally cited examples include the **Dreamweaver Cottage, Five Point Cottage, and Blue Pearl.** **[NHR]**
