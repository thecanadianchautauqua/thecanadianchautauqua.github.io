# The Hub-and-Spoke Source Map — Archives Beyond the Local Museum

Niagara-on-the-Lake and Chautauqua sit at the centre of a wheel whose spokes reach across the Niagara Peninsula, around Lake Ontario, into upstate New York and the Chautauqua County heartland, and back across the Atlantic to Scotland and England through the town's Loyalist and colonial lineages. The richest, least-duplicated material lives in these *external* repositories. This document maps them by geography, with what each holds and why it matters here.

> **Why look outward.** The local NOTL Museum has already catalogued most *local* holdings. The story's missing pieces are held elsewhere: the **Crooks** family papers in **Hamilton/Flamborough** and **Scotland**; the **steamers** in **Toronto** and **Liverpool**; the **1813 bombardment** at **Fort Niagara, NY**; the **movement's origin** in **Chautauqua County, NY**; and the **Simcoe** and **Butler** records in **England**. Each is a spoke below.

## Spoke 1 — The Niagara Peninsula (St. Catharines, Brock, Welland)

### St. Catharines Museum & Welland Canals Centre
**https://www.stcatharines.ca/en/arts-culture-and-events/collections.aspx** · online eMuseum via the same site.
~**800,000 records** — photographs, artifacts, maps, plans, and municipal/corporate/personal papers back to the late 19th century. Strong on the **Welland Canal, the Underground Railroad, shipping, and regional industry** that shaped Niagara's economy and the steamer trade feeding Chautauqua.

### Brock University Library — Archives & Special Collections (St. Catharines)
**https://brocku.ca/library/archives/** · digital exhibits **https://exhibits.library.brocku.ca/**
- **Friends of the Loyalist Collection** (formed 2002) — primary and microfilmed **United Empire Loyalist** documents, including **Butler's Rangers** material: https://www.uelac.org/Friends-Loyalist-Collection-BrockU/collection.html
- **Niagara Camp** WWI postcards; **George E. Curtis** Niagara photographs (c. 1860–70); **Historic Houses in Niagara**.

### Maritime History of the Great Lakes (hosted via Brock / Ontario)
**https://www.maritimehistoryofthegreatlakes.ca/** · images DB **https://images.maritimehistoryofthegreatlakes.ca/**
Deep records on the **Niagara Navigation Company** and the "C" steamers (*Chicora, Cibola, Chippewa, Corona, Cayuga*). Hosts the *Scanner* ship-history journal (e.g. the **CHIPPEWA** issue) and Barlow Cumberland's narrative.

## Spoke 2 — Head-of-the-Lake: Hamilton, Flamborough & Halton (the Crooks story)

### Flamborough Archives & Heritage Society
**https://www.flamboroughhistory.com/** (see the multi-part **"Crooks' Hollow"** history)
The afterlife of **James Crooks**, original owner of the future Chautauqua land: after the War of 1812 he resettled in **West Flamborough**, building the mills, store, and **first paper mill in Upper Canada (1826)** that became **Crooks' Hollow**.

### Library and Archives Canada — Crooks' Hollow cultural-landmark feature
**https://epe.lac-bac.gc.ca/100/205/301/ic/cdc/cultural_landmarks/crooks.htm**

### Genealogy: Waterloo Region Generations & UEL records
**https://generations.regionofwaterloo.ca/** — James Crooks lineage (b. Kilmarnock, Scotland 1778 → d. West Flamborough 1860), linking the Niagara estate to its **Scottish** origins.

*(Also relevant for the broader region: Hamilton Public Library Local History & Archives; Oakville Museum — note the VITA "OakvilleImages" collection; Halton heritage services.)*

## Spoke 3 — Toronto: provincial archives, library & the steamer companies

### Archives of Ontario (Toronto)
**https://www.archives.gov.on.ca/** · War of 1812 online feature: **https://www.archives.gov.on.ca/en/explore/online/1812/niagara-1813.aspx**
Holds the **Simcoe family fonds (F 47)** — the records of Newark's founding governor — and extensive Upper Canada land, military, and map records.

### Toronto Public Library — Baldwin Room / Digital Archive / eMuseum
**https://digitalarchive.tpl.ca/** · eMuseum **https://torontopubliclibrary.emuseum.com/**
170,000+ digitized items: a **portrait of John Graves Simcoe**, the published **Cruikshank *Correspondence of Lieut. Governor Simcoe***, lake-steamer and excursion imagery, and Ontario maps/postcards.

### University of Toronto — Discover Archives
**https://discoverarchives.library.utoronto.ca/** — Simcoe chronological files and related fonds.

### The Niagara Navigation Company (Toronto, est. 1877)
The Toronto firm whose steamers carried Chautauqua visitors. See Spoke 1 (Maritime History) and the **Niagara Gazette** (Spoke 5).

## Spoke 4 — Niagara Falls (Ontario)

### Niagara Falls (ON) Public Library — Historic Niagara Digital Collections
**https://nfpl.historicniagara.ca/** · index **https://nflibrary.ca/collections/historic-niagara-digital-collection**
Photographs, postcards, negatives, daguerreotypes, a newspaper index, and an art collection spanning the whole frontier, including NOTL, fires, and the lake steamers.

## Spoke 5 — The American Niagara frontier (Youngstown, Niagara Falls NY, Buffalo)

### Old Fort Niagara (Youngstown, NY)
**https://www.oldfortniagara.org/**
The fort *directly across the river*, from which the **two-day bombardment of 25–27 May 1813** levelled Fort George and the town. Origin point of the **Crooks family's** first mercantile house (~1788). Captured by the British **19 Dec 1813** after Newark's burning.

### Buffalo History Museum & Buffalo & Erie County Public Library (Buffalo, NY)
**https://buffalohistory.org/** · **https://www.buffalolib.org/**
War of 1812 collections covering the Niagara frontier campaign (and the retaliatory **burning of Buffalo**, Dec 1813). The **Niagara Gazette** (Niagara Falls, NY) carries the *Bicentennial Bits* series on the **Niagara Navigation Company** steamships.

### Niagara 1812 / Niagara Falls National Heritage Area
**https://discover1812.com/** · **https://www.discoverniagara.org/war-of-1812** — cross-border War of 1812 heritage and sites.

## Spoke 6 — Chautauqua County, NY: the parent movement (Lake Chautauqua, Jamestown)

### Chautauqua Institution Archives — Oliver Archives Center (Chautauqua, NY)
**https://www.chq.org/visit/resources/archives/** · online collections **https://chautauqua.pastperfectonline.com/** · digitized **https://digitizedcollections.archives.chq.org/**
The **original** Chautauqua, founded **August 1874** by **John Heyl Vincent** and **Lewis Miller** on **Lake Chautauqua near Jamestown, NY** — the direct model for the Niagara Assembly. Digitized **Chautauqua newspapers (1876–2006)** and **CLSC magazines (1880–1914)**; the **CLSC** is the program whose diploma was held by Niagara Assembly figure **Lewis C. Peake**.

### Library of Congress — Chautauqua collection
**https://memory.loc.gov/ammem/collections/chautauqua** — national documentation of the movement and its circuit "daughter Chautauquas."

## Spoke 7 — National & cross-border digitized libraries (primary texts)

### Library and Archives Canada (Ottawa)
**https://library-archives.canada.ca/eng** — **John Graves Simcoe fonds**; **Loyalist** records; military, land, and map collections.

### Canadiana (Canadian Research Knowledge Network)
**https://www.canadiana.ca/** — e.g. **William Kirby, *Annals of Niagara*** (1896): https://www.canadiana.ca/view/oocihm.09996

### Internet Archive & HathiTrust (full-text, public domain)
- **William Kirby, *Annals of Niagara*** (1896, Lundy's Lane Historical Society / Welland *Tribune*) — Internet Archive https://archive.org/details/annalsofniagara00kirb · HathiTrust https://babel.hathitrust.org/cgi/pt?id=hvd.32044081325326
- **E. A. Cruikshank (ed.), *The Correspondence of Lieut. Governor John Graves Simcoe*** — https://archive.org/details/correspondencewi02simc
- **Mrs. Simcoe's Diary** (with 237 illustrations, incl. her own sketches of the Niagara frontier) — https://archive.org/details/diaryofmrsjohngr00simcuoft
- **W. R. Riddell, *The Life of John Graves Simcoe*** — https://archive.org/details/lifeofjohngraves00ridd
- **William A. Smy, *An Annotated Nominal Roll of Butler's Rangers 1777–1784*** (Friends of the Loyalist Collection, Brock, 2004) — https://archive.org/details/annotatednominal0000smyw
- **John Heyl Vincent, *The Chautauqua Movement*** (1886) — https://archive.org/details/chautauquamovem00millgoog

### Dictionary of Canadian Biography
**http://www.biographi.ca/** — authoritative lives of **James Crooks**, **William Kirby**, and other figures: e.g. Crooks https://www.biographi.ca/en/bio/crooks_james_8E.html

### University of Michigan — William L. Clements Library (Ann Arbor)
**https://clements.umich.edu/** — Simcoe family correspondence (1770s–1810), a key American holding of UK/Canadian colonial papers.

## Spoke 8 — The Atlantic lineages: England, Scotland & Ireland

### Devon (England) — Devon Heritage Centre / former Devon Record Office (Exeter)
Holds the **original Simcoe Papers** (the family seat was in **Devonshire**); copies were transferred to the Archives of Ontario in **1952**. The Simcoe story is as much a Devon story as a Newark one.

### The National Archives (Kew, England)
**https://www.nationalarchives.gov.uk/** — the **Public Record Office** holds the bulk of **Butler's Rangers** government correspondence, military orders, **muster rolls**, and land-grant records, the Rangers having been a British provincial corps.

### Scotland — Crooks family origins (Kilmarnock, Ayrshire)
**James Crooks** was born in **Kilmarnock, Ayrshire** (1778), son of a shoemaker; the family's emigration via a **Fort Niagara** trading house ties Scotland → New York → Niagara → Hamilton in a single thread. (Records: ScotlandsPeople; National Records of Scotland.)

### Ireland & the Loyalist diaspora
Butler's Rangers and the broader Loyalist settlement drew on families of **Irish, Scots-Irish, English, and Palatine German** origin from the **Mohawk Valley** and **Pennsylvania frontier**; lineage research runs through the **United Empire Loyalists' Association of Canada** (https://uelac.ca/) and its **nominal rolls** (e.g. the annotated Butler's Rangers roll), with onward parish records in Ireland and the UK.

## How to use this map
1. **Resolve a person** → Dictionary of Canadian Biography, then the relevant local archive (Crooks → Flamborough; Simcoe → Archives of Ontario + Devon).
2. **Find an image** → start with the NOTL Museum and NOTL Heritage Portal (local), then St. Catharines Museum, TPL, Niagara Falls PL, Brock, and Maritime History of the Great Lakes (regional), then the Chautauqua Institution Archives (parent movement).
3. **Read a primary text** → Internet Archive / HathiTrust / Canadiana (Kirby, Simcoe correspondence, Smy's roll, Vincent's *Chautauqua Movement*).
4. **Chase a lineage abroad** → UELAC rolls → The National Archives (Kew) for Rangers; Devon for Simcoe; ScotlandsPeople for Crooks.
