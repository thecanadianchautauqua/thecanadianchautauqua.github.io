# Chautauqua & Niagara-on-the-Lake: A Documented History

A research collection documenting the history of **Niagara-on-the-Lake, Ontario** (NOTL), with a special focus on the **Chautauqua** neighbourhood in the Old Town. The project gathers timelines, narrative history, biographies, building and event records, photo-archive links with vivid descriptions, and a fully credited reference list.

> **Scope.** "Chautauqua" here means the historic *Niagara-on-the-Lake* Chautauqua / Mississauga Beach neighbourhood at Mississauga Point on Lake Ontario — **not** the Chautauqua Institution in Chautauqua County, New York (its inspiration), though the two are connected and cross-referenced where useful.

## Quick orientation

- **Niagara-on-the-Lake** was founded **1781** as *Butlersburg*, became **Newark**, the **first capital of Upper Canada (1792)**, was renamed **Niagara (1798)**, burned in the **War of 1812**, rebuilt, and adopted the postal name **"Niagara-on-the-Lake"** in the late 19th century. The Old Town is a designated **National Historic Site of Canada (2003)**.
- **Chautauqua** was an 1880s summer-assembly resort laid out as a **wheel of streets radiating from Circle Street**, with a 4,000-seat amphitheatre and lakeside hotels. The Niagara Assembly opened its first season in **1888**, went bankrupt by **1894**, and the hotel (renamed the **Strathcona**) burned on **27 August 1909**. The radial plan and many cottages survive as a beloved residential neighbourhood today.

## Conventions & sourcing

- Every factual claim is followed by a citation pointer; full URLs live in [references/references.md](references/references.md).
- Where sources disagree (e.g., **James** vs **William Crooks**, or the exact acreage), the conflict is flagged inline rather than silently resolved.
- Photo entries give the **host institution, a direct link, and a description** of what the image shows so the collection is useful even before the image loads.
